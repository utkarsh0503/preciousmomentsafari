Mahela National Park, one of the most beautiful and untouched parts of Tanzania, is an absolute adventure for nature and wildlife lovers. This park is located in the southern part of the country, where it boasts various geographical features, such as thick rainforests and wide savanna prairies. Travelers can experience the peacefulness away from the buzz of everyday life while marveling at some of Tanzania’s best-kept secrets.

FAQs – MAHELA NATIONAL PARK SAFARI

What animals can be seen in Mahela National Park?

Mahela National Park is home to a diverse range of wildlife. Common sightings include elephants, lions, leopards, giraffes, zebras, and various antelope species. The park also hosts rare species such as the African wild dog and several primate species like baboons and monkeys. For bird enthusiasts, the park offers an abundance of species, including the vibrant lilac-breasted roller and the endangered African grey parrot..

Can I see lions during my safari in Mahela National Park?

Lions are one of the major predators in Mahela National Park, and while sightings can never be guaranteed, the chances of encountering a pride of lions are relatively high. The park’s diverse habitats offer a perfect setting for lions to hunt and rest, and safari-goers have often reported memorable sightings of these magnificent animals.

Are there elephants in Mahela National Park?

Yes, Mahela National Park is famous for its thriving population of elephants. Visitors often spot herds of elephants roaming freely across the park’s savannah and forests. These majestic creatures are a major attraction for safari-goers, providing a captivating experience as they engage in their natural behaviors.

Is Mahela National Park a good destination for birdwatching?

Absolutely! With over 400 bird species, Mahela National Park is a dream come true for birdwatchers. From the majestic crowned cranes and African fish eagles to the colorful bee-eaters and kingfishers, the park’s rich birdlife makes it one of the best birdwatching spots in Tanzania. The wetland areas, in particular, attract a variety of waterfowl and migratory birds, adding to the park’s appeal for avian enthusiasts.

What is the best time of year to visit Mahela National Park?

The best time to visit Mahela National Park is during the dry season, which runs from June to October. During this period, animals gather around the remaining water sources, making wildlife sightings more frequent and easier to spot. The park’s lush green landscapes during the wet season (November to April) also offer a completely different experience, especially for those interested in photography and birdwatching.

WHAT TO DO

Top Activities in Mahela National Park

Game Drives

Explore Mahela’s diverse landscapes on a game drive. Spot elephants, lions, giraffes, and more as you journey through forests and savannas. Morning and afternoon drives offer the best wildlife sightings.

Birdwatching

Mahela is a haven for birdwatchers, with over 400 bird species. From African Fish Eagles to vibrant Lilac-breasted Rollers, birdwatching here is exceptional, especially around wetlands and forests.

Walking Safari

Get closer to nature with a walking safari. Led by experienced guides, this activity allows you to discover the park’s flora, fauna, and animal tracks up close, offering a unique and educational experience.

Photography Safaris

Capture stunning wildlife and landscapes during your photography safari. The park’s diverse environments offer ample opportunities for both wildlife and landscape photography, especially at sunrise and sunset.
