Discover Ngorongoro Crater with Precious Moments Safari

Nestled in the heart of Tanzania’s northern circuit, the Ngorongoro Crater is one of the world’s most iconic natural wonders. Often referred to as the “Garden of Eden,” this UNESCO World Heritage Site offers a breathtaking and diverse landscape. The crater, formed millions of years ago by a massive volcanic eruption, is home to an astonishing variety of wildlife and ecosystems. Its floor, rich with lush greenery and savannah plains, is an unparalleled sanctuary for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned safari-goer, Ngorongoro is sure to leave you in awe.

Why You Should Visit Ngorongoro Crater?.

Unmatched Wildlife Diversity: The crater boasts an impressive array of wildlife, including the “Big Five” (lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhinoceros). The fertile ground and water supply make it a year-round home for thousands of animals. Stunning Landscapes: The dramatic scenery, with its steep crater walls, lush highlands, and sprawling grasslands, makes for incredible photo opportunities at every turn. You’ll witness the crater floor from a stunning viewpoint, creating memories that will last a lifetime. Easy Wildlife Viewing: The animals in Ngorongoro Crater are relatively easy to spot as they congregate around water sources. The close proximity of the wildlife to visitors ensures excellent game viewing, making this one of the best places for a safari. Conservation and Sustainability: The Ngorongoro Conservation Area is a model for sustainable tourism and conservation efforts, ensuring that the wildlife and natural beauty of the area are preserved for future generations. Accessibility: Ngorongoro is easily accessible from Arusha, making it an excellent destination for travelers seeking a short but unforgettable safari experience.

FAQs – NGORONGORO CRATER

What is the best time to visit Ngorongoro Crater?

The best time to visit Ngorongoro is during the dry season, from June to October, when wildlife viewing is at its peak. The dry season ensures that the animals gather around waterholes, offering a higher chance of spotting the Big Five..

How do I get to Ngorongoro Crater?

Ngorongoro Crater is located about 180 km from Arusha. You can easily reach the crater by car or 4×4, with the journey taking about 3 to 4 hours. Many travelers combine their Ngorongoro visit with a safari in Serengeti, Tarangire, or Lake Manyara National Park.

What animals can I see in Ngorongoro Crater?

Ngorongoro is home to a vast range of wildlife, including lions, elephants, buffaloes, zebras, wildebeest, hyenas, leopards, and rhinoceroses. The crater’s unique ecosystem supports this incredible diversity, and visitors often enjoy the rare opportunity to see these animals in close proximity.

Is Ngorongoro Crater suitable for families?

Yes, Ngorongoro Crater is an ideal destination for families. The ease of wildlife viewing and the abundance of animal species make it a perfect location for a family safari. Children will be fascinated by the wildlife, and there are also family-friendly accommodations available near the crater.

How long should I stay in Ngorongoro Crater?

Most visitors spend a full day in Ngorongoro Crater, though you can stay longer to explore the surrounding areas. A one-day safari is typically sufficient for a game drive and a visit to the crater’s viewpoints, but if you have more time, you can also explore the Ngorongoro Highlands.

WHAT MOST PEOPLE LIKE

Most popular animals found in
Ngorongoro Crater

Ngorongoro Crater is home to a dense population of wildlife, making it one of the most unique safari destinations in Africa. The crater hosts thousands of animals within its walls, offering an exceptional chance to see a wide range of species in one location. Large herds of wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles dominate the grasslands, while predators like lions, cheetahs, hyenas, and leopards maintain the natural balance. The crater is also a stronghold for the endangered black rhinoceros and supports a healthy number of elephants, buffaloes, and hippos. Other common sightings include warthogs, jackals, servals, elands, waterbucks, and bushbucks. Birdwatchers will be pleased with over 500 bird species recorded, including flamingos, ostriches, crowned cranes, and kori bustards
