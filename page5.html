6-Day Kilimanjaro Marangu Route

6-Day Kilimanjaro Marangu Route

Tour Overview

The 'Coca Cola' route or Marangu is the most frequented route on Kilimanjaro. This route is famous for offering huts as accommodation which are easier to access than other mountain climbing hotels. This makes it a better option for hiking during the rainy season.. The route starts at the southeastern side of Mt Kilimanjaro. Our base at Moshi town is very close to the entrance of the route, Marangu Gate Kilimanjaro national park head quarter. It takes approximately 1.2 hours to get there. This route features an elevation that is dotted with forests, zone of tropical rain, and perpetual ice, as well as picturesque African scenery.

Day 0 - Arrivals

As soon as you land at Kilimanjaro Airport JRO, you will meet with me for a short drive of about an hour to Moshi Town, where you will attend an orientation about your hotel. Spend the remainder of the day at the Hotel or explore Moshi town, at your leisure. My assistance includes detailing the specifics of your trek, which requires me to check your trekking gears. If any gear is missing, I will assist you in renting those pieces at the local rental shops.

Day to Day Trip

Day 1: Moshi to Marangu Gate to Mandara Hut

After breakfast, we will set off for Marangu gate. Once we have registered with the park authorities, our climb will officially begin with a four-hour walk to the Mandara hut. Our guides will constantly repeat the phrase, “pole pole, hakuna matata”, which means “proceed slowly, no stress” in Swahili. We will pass through dense tropical rainforest zone and will finally spend the night at Mandara Hut. The Mandara Hut is equipped with sleeping bunks, running water and flush toilets.

2720m

Day 2 Mandara Hutto Horombo hut

Today's journey starts with a brief trek through the climbing zone and advances into the moorland zone at approximately 3,000m. This elevation is attainable within a six hour walk. We wish to see the breathtaking views of the mountain peaks that are possible at this altitude. We wish to have some rest and get some sleep at Horombo Hut (3,720m) which is situated in a basin that is encompassed by giant lobelia which grows to 3m in height and groundsel, or Senecio Kilimanjari, that can reach 5m. The flora is characteristic of the semi alpine zone that we are in. Bathing facilities areprovided with flowing water. Our group shall take a bowl with hot washing water while our cook gets ready with the dinner.

Day 3: Acclimitization Day at Horombo Hut

There's no need to pack your bags today as you will be spending a second night at Horombo. Your guide will take you on an acclimatization stroll to Zebrarocks and the bottom of Mawenzi Peak where splendid views of Kibo capturing the saddle will be waiting for you. You may also spend the rest of the day at camp, relaxing and recovering from your previous adventures.

Day 4: Horombo hutto Kibo hut

We begin the gradual walk into the moorland, the plants become barren as we reach the alpine zone which looks similar to a desert (around 4000 meters). For today, the hike will be at a slower pace to allow for easier acclimatization, but we need to monitor for signs of altitude sickness. The climb will take you between the Kibo and Mawenzi peaks – you’ll need the rest to load up on energy for the summit hike.

Day 5: Kibo Hut to Summitto Horombo Hut

On the hike to Uhuru Peak, the last part of the climb to Uhuru Peak occurs after midnight. First, you have the daunting task of completing a five hour ascent to Gillman’s Point on the rim of the crater. It is the easiest of the three routes up the crater, but not exactly a walk in the park. William’s Point is the first major stop and rests at an astounding 5000 meters. It takes about two hours to reach the point from Kibo hut. After that, you have a further thirty minutes of hiking before you start the rocky switchbacks that lead to Gillman’s Point. The altitude mark here is 5681 meters. The hike from Gilman’s to Uhuru Peak is gradual and not tough. Circumstances caused by the altitude render the entire journey lengthy and exhausting. You’ll take two hours to hike along the crater rim. You’ll notice that the views on the way down are nothing if not breathtaking. Stop at Kibo Hut to grab a bite before heading onto Horombo Hut which you reach during day. Following that, enjoy the last few nights spent on the mountain.

Day 6: Horombo Hut to Marangu gate

After taking in the view from Uhuru Peak and soaking in the experience, you are well-rested and ready to tackle the journey towards civilization. The return trip is easier, and you’ll be hiking through mesmerizing moorland which will take you to Mandara Hut. After Mandara Hut, it’s just a steep walk through a serene forest pathway, which leads you directly to the National Park gate at Marangu. Marangu Gate is equipped with everything you require, including your summit certificates. Don’t forget to show gratitude towards your guide, cook, and porters for their help during the hike. Now it’s time to let loose and enjoy the well-deserved celebration!
